# DoingOK — Executive Summary

## The Problem

Millions of older adults, people with disabilities, and individuals managing mental health conditions live alone or with limited support. For many, a single missed call, forgotten check-in, or medical episode can go undetected for hours or days — sometimes with devastating consequences. Families live with this worry. Community organizations see the gaps firsthand. And existing alert systems — personal emergency response devices, neighbor check-ins — depend on the person being able to act in a crisis, or on someone already knowing to look.

## What DoingOK Does

DoingOK is a nonprofit mobile app that gives vulnerable individuals a daily digital check-in. Each day, the user receives a prompt: a simple "I'm OK" tap. If they don't respond within their chosen time window, the system automatically begins reaching out — first to the user directly, then to their trusted contacts in priority order, and then, if contacts are unreachable, to a network of trained community volunteers.

The escalation is automatic, sequenced, and configurable. No one has to remember to check. No one has to make judgment calls under pressure. The system handles the routine so that human attention is focused where it matters most — on people who actually need it.

## Who We Serve

DoingOK is designed for adults who want to maintain independence while giving their families peace of mind. Primary users include seniors living alone, adults with disabilities, and individuals in recovery or managing chronic mental health conditions. Their trusted contacts — family members, friends, care coordinators — are also part of the system, notified and empowered to respond.

## Where Community Organizations Fit

Trusted contacts and trained volunteers are the heart of DoingOK's escalation model. We are actively building partnerships with:

- **Healthcare and social service providers** who work with eligible individuals and can refer them to the platform
- **Volunteer organizations** whose members can serve as trained responders in the escalation chain when a user's personal contacts are unreachable
- **Community advocates** who can help us reach underserved populations who would benefit most

A partnership with DoingOK does not require technical integration. For referral partners, it is as simple as connecting eligible individuals to the app. For volunteer-based organizations, it means contributing trained, compassionate responders to a structured escalation network — with clear protocols, limited time commitment, and direct community impact.

## Where We Are

DoingOK is in active development, with a working mobile application (iOS and Android), a complete backend alert system, and a beta launch planned for later this year. Our Terms of Service are being finalized and HIPAA compliance requirements are under review with our legal team.

We are seeking partner organizations now — before launch — so that we can build referral pipelines, shape volunteer training requirements collaboratively, and ensure the platform reflects the realities of the communities it serves.

## Be Part of the Beta

Early partnership means early impact. We are inviting a small number of organizations to participate in our beta program, contributing a group of willing clients, members, or constituents to help us test the platform in real-world conditions before public launch.

Beta participants receive:
- White-glove onboarding support for their users
- A direct line to the DoingOK team to report feedback and shape feature priorities
- Recognition as a founding partner at launch

Beta users help us answer the questions that only real-world use can reveal: Does the daily prompt feel right? Is the escalation timing appropriate? Are there populations or use cases we haven't anticipated? Their experience will directly improve the platform for everyone who comes after them.

If your organization serves individuals who would benefit from daily wellness monitoring and you are open to participating in a structured pilot, we want to hear from you.
